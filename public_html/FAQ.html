What is FestivalWare?

FestivalWare is intended to showcase and promote American music and has been conducted across the nation since 1898. It is a one-day event that provides school-aged students with a venue in which to perform and be professionally judged. Students are evaluated according to specific criteria (see below).  FestivalWare is not a competition; each student is rated on the merits of their individual performance.

Who sponsors FestivalWare?

The FestivalWare is a nationwide program provided through the National Federation of Music Clubs (N.F.M.C.). The local chapter of the Music Teachers National Association, The Demo Music Teachers Association, organizes and moderates the event.

What does DMTA stand for?

The Demo Music Teachers Association.

What does NFMC stand for?

The National Federation of Music Clubs.

How long has FestivalWare been around?

The FestivalWare has been conducted across the nation since 1898.

Who can participate in FestivalWare?

Any student whose teacher has registered for the Festival may participate.

How much does it cost to participate?

Teachers who are DMTA members pay a $14.00 registration fee. Non-DMTA teachers pay a $30.00 registration fee. This fee registers the teacher with the NFMC, and allows the teacher to register up to 20 students. Teachers (whether DMTA members or not) that register more than 20 students pay an additional 70 cents per student over 20 students.

All teachers (DMTA members or not) pay $10.00 per student for each event (this cost is usually passed on to the student).

Where is FestivalWare being held?

This year's FestivalWare will be held at UNLV's Music Building for piano students.

All other instruments and voice performances will be held at another location (to be announced later).

Who pays the judges and personnel involved with FestivalWare?

The FestivalWare is completely volunteer-based. The judges and personnel organizing and working the festival are not paid. All proceeds from the Festival benefit the DMTA and are used to promote music teachers and programs throughout the valley.

I am a teacher. What will I be expected to do?

 You are expected to follow the rules and regulations found in the National Federation of Music Clubs Festival Bulletin 2004-2006, the information found on this web site, and any information that is found in the DMTA monthly newsletter pertaining to FestivalWare. Because FestivalWare is completely volunteer-driven, you are expected to do your part by actively serving on one of the Festival committees, as well as help on Festival day, all day. Part of your duties on Festival day will be to serve as a judge. Teachers that are not entering students are always welcome to help.

I am a student. What will I be expected to do?

 You are expected to work closely with your teacher to prepare for FestivalWare. The rules and regulations found within the National Federation of Music Clubs Festival Bulletin 2004-2006, will help you and your teacher determine which repertoire you will play and will help you understand what your responsibilities are as a participant.

I am a parent of a participant. What will I be expected to do?

 You have a very special responsibility to help your child do his or her best by helping your child prepare for lessons. Your child's teacher has many ideas for implementing good practice skills and should be followed faithfully. Help your child arrive on time for lessons, ready to work with their teachers. On festival day, students will need to arrive 20 minutes prior to their scheduled performance. Of course, if you would like to help out in other ways, please contact us!

I am an DMTA teacher. How do I register my students?

Regardless of membership, all registration and scheduling is done through the FestivalWare website: www.lvjrfest.org. Click on the Register your Studio link to go to the registration page. Once you have registered your studio, you then pay your registration fee by sending a check in the amount listed to the address given on the Acceptance page.

I'm a teacher, and I don't belong to the DMTA or NFMC. Can my students still participate?

Absolutely. Since FestivalWare is sponsored by the NFMC, a nominal fee of $14.00 is charged to register all teachers with that organization. Teachers that are not members of DMTA are charged an additional nominal fee ($16.00, for a total of $30.00) to handle administrative costs.

I am a student. How do I register?

Sorry, but students can't register themselves. Talk to your teacher about FestivalWare. If they haven't registered as a teacher, have them visit the web site for more information. Once they are registered, they can register you. If you don't have a teacher, contact the Demo Music Teacher's Association, who will help you find a teacher.

My child is participating in FestivalWare for the first time. What should I expect on performance day?

  Your child will need to arrive at least 20 minutes prior to the scheduled performance time (which your child's teacher will give you). Follow the signs in the hallways to locate your performance room. Each performance room has a room monitor, who will open the door for you when it is your turn. The judge will call your child up to play. Your child will need to bring the original sheet music for the judges. Absolutely no photocopies are permitted! There may be other people in the room; visitors and observers are permitted, as long as they are quiet. After your child's performance, the monitor will open the door and you may exit to retrieve your participation certificate and your sheet music.

What are the judging criteria?

Please see the sample Judging Sheet. This sheet will be filled out by each judge for each student's performance.

What are the rules for participating?

The FestivalWare has many rules and regulations which must be followed exactly.  This information is printed in the National Federation of Music Clubs Festival Bulletin 2004-2006, which will be available at the DMTA opening social, each DMTA Board meeting, and Bonnie's Music Shoppe. The fee for this bulletin is $7.50. 

What do the students receive for participating?

Students receive a certificate of participation, as well as a sense of completing something important and congratulations from their family, friends, and teachers.  They also accumulate points based on their ratings, which are applied toward the earning of gold cups (trophies).  These points are never lost even if the student skips a year.
